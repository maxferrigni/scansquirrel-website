Questions to ask any service before you hand over your photos.

Regardless of who you use, these are worth knowing the answers to upfront.

  • → Where does the scanning actually happen? A local address on the website doesn't always mean local processing.
  • → What is the all-in price? Add up shipping both ways, digital delivery fees, and any subscription that auto-starts after a free trial.
  • → What happens once they have your photos? Some services have no-refund policies the moment they receive your items. Read the fine print before you ship.
  • → How do you get your digital files? A thumb drive or DVD is a physical object that can fail. Make sure you understand exactly how you'll access your photos long-term.
  • → Who do you call if something looks wrong? A local person you can reach is a very different situation from a support ticket.
  • → Is there a monthly subscription involved? Cloud storage upsells that auto-enroll after a free trial are common. Check before you check out.
  • → What DPI do they scan at? 600 DPI is the archival standard for prints. Lower DPI means lower resolution — fine for sharing on a phone, not ideal for printing large.
