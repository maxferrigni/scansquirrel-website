The people behind ScanSquirrel

Meet the Squirrels.

Every Squirrel is a trained, local specialist who handles your collection personally — from pickup to delivery. Not a warehouse. Not a mail-away box. A neighbor who's accountable to you by name.

Every Squirrel lives in the neighborhood they serve. That's not a detail — it's the whole product.

For customers

Why a network beats a warehouse.

Companies like Legacybox put your irreplaceable photos in a box and ship them to a facility hundreds of miles away, where strangers you'll never meet handle them in bulk. We do the opposite. ScanSquirrel is a network of hardworking local people — focused entirely on photos, documents, and the things that matter most — who come to you and do the careful work by hand.

Become a Squirrel

Could you be a Squirrel?

We're growing a network of people who care about this the way we do. You don't need experience — just the right instincts. If this sounds like you, we'd love to hear from you.

  • → You're local. You'd serve the neighborhood you actually live in — same streets, same history, same fire risk. That personal connection is the whole point.
  • → You're organized and careful. The work is detailed and hands-on. People are trusting you with irreplaceable things, so the little stuff matters.
  • → You're willing to learn. No tech background needed. If you're up for being trained properly, we'll teach you everything — scanning, handling, the software, all of it.
  • → You like helping people. At the end of the day this is about showing up for your neighbors and protecting what matters to them.

A good side hustle

Meaningful work, on your own schedule.

Being a Squirrel is hands-on, flexible work helping people in your own neighborhood protect what matters to them. It's the kind of side hustle that actually feels good to do — you finish a job knowing you saved someone's family memories.

You don't need to be a tech expert or own any special equipment. If you're organized, careful, and willing to learn, I'll personally train you on how to do this work the right way — the scanning, the handling, the software, all of it.

We're still early and figuring things out as we grow, so the best way to learn more is simply to reach out. No application, no pressure — just a conversation about whether it's a fit.
