Pricing

How much does it cost?

+

One dollar per photo. That's it. A typical shoebox of around 200 photos runs about $200. There are no setup fees, no hidden charges, no subscriptions, and nothing gets added at checkout.

The fireproof storage case is optional at $50 — I include it because I think it's worth it, but it's your call.

Are there any hidden fees?

+

No. The price I quote is the price you pay. No cloud storage subscriptions. No digital download fees. No shipping charges. No "protection plan" upsells. You pay per photo, and that includes everything — scanning, enhancement, delivery to your phone, and return of your originals.

Do you offer discounts for large collections?

+

If you have a very large collection — multiple shoeboxes, estate archives, that kind of thing — reach out and we can talk. I'd rather work something out than have you sit on a box of photos for another decade.

When do I pay?

+

After pickup, once I've counted and inventoried everything, I'll give you the exact total. Payment is due when I return your originals. I accept Venmo, Zelle, cash, and card.

Is the consultation really free?

+

Yes. We can talk on the phone, text, or I can come by and take a look at what you have. No charge, no pressure, no obligation. If it turns out you only have 30 photos and you'd rather do it yourself, I'll tell you that.

The Process

Do you actually come to my house?

+

Yes. I come to you, we go through your collection together, I take everything back to my studio in Eagle Rock, and I return it to you when it's done. Your photos never get handed off to a stranger at a counter or dropped in a shipping box.

How long does it take?

+

Typically 48 hours from pickup to delivery. For very large collections it may be a bit longer — I'll always give you a realistic timeframe before I start.

What exactly happens to my photos while they're with you?

+

I sort them by size and era, scan each one at 600 DPI — front and back — then run AI enhancement to reverse fading and sharpen faces. I embed dates and names in the file metadata so everything lands in the right spot on your timeline. Then I organize the originals, label them, and pack them into a fireproof case before returning them to you.

Do I need to sort or prepare my photos before you arrive?

+

No. Dump them in a box, leave them in the albums, pile them on the table — I've seen everything. Part of what you're paying for is the sorting and organization. The messier they are, the more satisfying the result.

What if I'm not happy with the results?

+

Tell me. I'm ten minutes away. We figure it out together. That's the advantage of working with a person instead of a warehouse — if something doesn't look right, I can rescan it, adjust the enhancement, or redo a batch. I don't disappear after delivery.

Formats

What kinds of photos do you scan?

+

Standard prints in any size — 3×5, 4×6, 5×7, 8×10, and everything in between including the odd non-standard sizes from the 1970s and 80s. Front and back on every photo.

Can you scan slides and negatives?

+

Yes. 35mm slides, 35mm negatives, and most other common film formats. Slides and negatives are priced at $1.50 each. If you're not sure what format you have, just send me a photo and I'll tell you.

Can you scan photos that are still in albums?

+

It depends on the album. Some photos come out cleanly; others are glued in ways that make removal risky. I'll look at your albums and give you an honest answer before touching anything. I won't pull a photo out of an album if there's a real chance of damaging it.

My photos are really old and faded. Can anything be done?

+

Usually yes. AI enhancement can recover a lot from faded, yellowed, or slightly damaged prints — reversed colors, sharpened faces, corrected exposure. I'll show you a before and after before delivering the full set so you know exactly what you're getting.

Do you scan oversized photos or framed prints?

+

Reach out and tell me what you have. Oversized prints are doable in many cases. Framed photos depend on whether they can be safely removed — I won't force anything out of a frame.

Trust & Safety

How do I know my photos are safe with you?

+

I live and work in Eagle Rock. My name is Max Ferrigni. You can look me up, ask your neighbors, check my references. I handle your photos the same way I'd want someone to handle mine — carefully, personally, and with the understanding that they can't be replaced.

Your photos stay in Los Angeles the entire time. They don't go into a shipping box, they don't get handed to a warehouse, and they don't pass through multiple sets of hands.

What happens if something gets damaged?

+

I'll tell you immediately and we'll figure it out together. I take this seriously. I photograph and inventory everything at pickup specifically so there's a clear record of what I received and what condition it was in. In over a year of doing this, I haven't damaged a photo — but if I ever do, I'm not going to hide it.

Do you share or use my photos for anything?

+

Only with your explicit permission. The sample photos on the website are there because the owners agreed to share them. Your collection is yours. I don't use customer photos in marketing, I don't post them anywhere, and I delete my working copies after delivery.

Do I get my originals back?

+

Always. Your originals come back organized, labeled by era or event, and stored in a fireproof, waterproof case. You end up with everything better than you started — physically organized AND digitally accessible.

Delivery

How do I get my digital photos?

+

I send you a link that drops everything directly into your Google Photos or Apple Photos library. No thumb drive to lose, no DVD to find a player for. Your photos show up on your phone, organized by date, and face recognition starts working automatically.

What format are the files?

+

JPEG for the enhanced versions delivered to your phone. I also produce 600 DPI archival masters — these are the full-resolution files you can use for large prints or long-term preservation. Both come with embedded metadata: dates, names, and event labels where I can determine them.

Do I need to subscribe to anything to access my photos?

+

No. Google Photos and Apple Photos both have free tiers that are more than enough for a typical family archive. You don't need to sign up for anything I run or pay any recurring fees.

Can I get a thumb drive instead?

+

Yes, just ask. I can deliver on a thumb drive if you prefer physical media. I'd still recommend the phone delivery as the primary — thumb drives get lost and fail over time. But having both isn't a bad idea for a large collection.
