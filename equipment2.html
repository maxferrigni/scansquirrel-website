Equipment & Tech

I get this question a lot: what do you actually use? Here is the short answer. These are the scanners, software, and storage tools I trust with other people's irreplaceable photos. I am a big fan of all of them.

Photo Scanners

For shoeboxes, photo albums, loose prints - the stuff most people have a lot of - speed and consistency matter more than anything.

My Favorite

Epson FastFoto FF-680W

This is the workhorse. It scans a stack of photos at 600 DPI faster than you can blink, captures the front and back of every photo, and the image quality is genuinely excellent. I am a big fan. If you only buy one scanner, this is the one.

For The Tricky Stuff

A More Powerful Flatbed

For oversized photos, fragile originals, frames, and anything that should not go through a feeder, I use a high-quality flatbed scanner. It is slower but it is the right tool for delicate or unusual pieces.

Slides & Negatives

Slides and negatives are a different world. They need a dedicated film scanner with much higher resolution, infrared dust removal, and proper color calibration. The cheap stuff will not cut it.

My Favorite So Far

Plustek OpticFilm 135i Ai

This is what I reach for first for slides and negatives. Auto-feeding, sharp, fast for a film scanner, and the image quality looks outstanding. I have not tested it too extensively yet, but so far I really like it.

~$799 View on Amazon
Also Excellent

Plustek OpticFilm 8300i Ai

I have also used the 8300i Ai - manual feed, but the image quality is right up there with the 135i. A great choice if you only have a small batch of slides or negatives and you do not need the auto-feeder.

~$539 View on Amazon

AI Enhancement

This is the part that genuinely amazes people. Once your photos are scanned, I run them through a custom-built enhancement pipeline. Faded colors come back. Faces sharpen. Damaged spots get repaired. Dates and names get embedded automatically - so when your photos arrive in your iPhone Photos or Google Photos, they land in the right spot on your timeline. No sorting. No dragging. No "what year was this?" - they just appear where they belong.

That is the magic moment. You open your phone and a photo from 1978 shows up in your timeline like it was always there. You scroll your year and your dad is suddenly young again, your kids are small, and the memories come back in order. Your collection becomes part of your life again, not a box in the closet.

The original archival scan is always preserved untouched. Enhancements are a separate file, so nothing about the original is ever lost.
