About your Eastside Squirrel

I work the Eastside because I live the Eastside. Eagle Rock isn't Highland Park. Highland Park isn't Silver Lake. You drive ten minutes and everything changes. Most of the city flattens out. This part doesn't.

The staircases

Hidden in the hills

Old public staircases all over the hills. A hundred years old, some of them, stuck between houses where you'd never think to look. My wife and I walk them. You go up what looks like somebody's private steps and pop out looking at the whole basin.

The wildlife

You just have to look up

Coyotes at dusk. Hawks over Mount Washington. Owls — you hear them before you ever see them. There's a hawk's nest near me I've been watching for a while now.

People think you've gotta leave the city for this stuff. You don't.

The reservoir

The reservoir

I love the Silver Lake reservoir, especially the dog parks.

The stadium

Right up the hill

Dodger Stadium sits right in the middle of all this, tucked in the hills between Echo Park, Silver Lake, and Elysian Park. Game nights you can hear the crowd from half the neighborhoods on this page.

The light

A different kind of warm

It gets hot here. He finds a warm patch on the floor and that's it for him for the day.

The fires

January 2025 changed everything

The Eaton and Palisades fires burned through neighborhoods just like ours. Families lost everything — including photos that can never be replaced. That's what made me start doing this. Most of us have a shoebox in a closet that would be gone in twenty minutes.

This is home

Your photos stay on the Eastside

Your photos came out of neighborhoods like these. Backyards, front steps, the reservoir, some kid's birthday in a Highland Park kitchen. Hand them to me and they don't get shipped across the country — they stay right here. I scan them and I hand them back.
