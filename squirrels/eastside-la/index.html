I scan photos here because I live here

I work the Eastside because I live the Eastside. What I love about it is that it's not one place. Eagle Rock isn't Highland Park. Highland Park isn't Silver Lake. You can drive ten minutes and the whole feel changes — the houses, the trees, the light, the people on the sidewalk. Most of the city flattens out. This part doesn't.

The staircases

Hidden in the hills

There are hidden staircases all over these hills. Public ones, built a hundred years ago, tucked between houses where you'd never expect them. My wife and I walk them. You climb a flight that looks like someone's private steps and come out on a street with a view of the whole basin.

I take pictures of them. I can't help it.

The wildlife

You just have to look up

If you pay attention, this place is full of animals. Coyotes at dusk. Hawks riding the thermals over Mount Washington. Owls you hear before you see. There's a hawk's nest I've been watching.

People think you have to leave the city to see this. You don't. You just have to look up.

The reservoir

Where the neighborhood shows up

I like walking around the Silver Lake reservoir. The dog parks there. It's one of those places where the whole neighborhood shows up and you end up knowing faces even if you don't know names.

The light

A different kind of warm

It gets hot here. But it's a different hot than the Valley or the flatter parts of the city — there's less concrete, more hillside, more trees, so the heat sits differently. It's warm without that baked, hard-surface feel. The light is good. My cat agrees — she finds the warmest patch in the house and stays there all afternoon.
