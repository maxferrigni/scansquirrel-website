Three kinds of services, plainly described.

Before the numbers, it helps to understand what you're actually choosing between.

The warehouse

National mail-in services

Ship your irreplaceable photos to a 110,000 sq ft facility in Tennessee. They process thousands of orders a day. You get a thumb drive back in 3–8 weeks. Fine if you're not picky. Impersonal by design. No one there knows your grandmother's name.

Scale over care
The local shop

Drop-off / DVD services

Drop your photos at a counter. Pick up a DVD or USB stick when they call. Local is better than shipping across the country — but a DVD can scratch, a USB drive can fail, and the person behind the counter doesn't know what's in your collection.

Local but impersonal
ScanSquirrel

Your neighbor, with a scanner

Your Squirrel comes to you. You go through your photos together. Everything gets scanned at 600 DPI with AI enhancement and delivered directly to your Google or Apple Photos. Your Squirrel knows your name, knows your neighborhood, and works until you're happy. Your photos never leave your city.

Personal. Modern. Local.

A USB stick is not a plan.

How your digital files get delivered matters more than most people realize — and it's where older local services quietly fall short.

The old way

DVD · USB stick · CD

Physical media fails. DVDs scratch. USB drives corrupt. They get lost in a drawer. You paid to digitize your memories and ended up with a new single point of failure.

One bad day and it's gone
The ScanSquirrel way

Your photos land in the app you already use every day. They show up as Memories. You can share them in two taps. No thumb drive to lose. No disc to scratch. No subscription to cancel.

The honest version.

Mail-in services work. If you're in a town with no local option, shipping your photos to a reputable service is a reasonable choice.

But wherever there's a Squirrel near you — and we're growing — there is no reason to ship your irreplaceable family photos to a warehouse across the country, wait six weeks, pay for a subscription you'll forget to cancel, and get back a USB stick you'll lose in a drawer.

You have a local option. Someone from your neighborhood who comes to your house, goes through your collection with you, uses better technology than any warehouse, delivers everything directly to your phone in 48 hours, and actually cares whether you love the result.
